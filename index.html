National Tsing Hua University

NTHU was first founded in Beijing. After the Kuomintang retreated to Taiwan in 1949 following defeat by the Communist Party of China in the Chinese Civil War, NTHU was re-established in Hsinchu City in 1956.

Today, both Tsinghua universities have deep mutual cooperation, including an establishment of Tsinghua Strait Research Institute, dual degree program, MOOC program, and academic exchange program.

National Chiao Tung University

NCTU is a public research university located in Hsinchu, Taiwan. Established in 1896 as Nanyang Public School by an imperial edict of the Guangxu Emperor, it is one of Taiwan's leading universities.

After the Chinese Civil War, NCTU was re-established by former Chiao Tung University faculty and alumni in 1958. It is selected by Taiwan's Ministry of Education to pursue all-around excellence with "versatile international competitiveness to continuously strengthen international academic influence and visibility".

Tunghai University

THU was founded by Methodist missionaries in 1955 as a comprehensive university, the first private university and the second oldest university in Taiwan.

On the grounds, the Luce Memorial Chapel (designed by noted architects Chen Chi-kwan and I. M. Pei) is a local landmark. The logo of the university contains a cross in reference to the statement in the founding documents that it was "founded in the love of Jesus," and the three linked circles refer to the Holy Trinity as well as the motto, "Truth, Faith, Deeds".
